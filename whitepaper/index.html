1. Introduction

$BURNA is an ERC-20 token intended to support community programs, token-gated access, and on-chain incentives. The project emphasizes verification (contract-first communication) and publicly documented program rules.

2. Objectives

  • Support token-gated access and community participation.
  • Run incentive programs designed to be verifiable on-chain.
  • Introduce governance in phases, with documented controls.

3. Context

  • Public token projects frequently face impersonation and link spoofing.
  • Community programs require clear eligibility criteria and auditability.
  • Transparency improves trust and reduces operational risk.

4. Proposed approach

  • Contract-first communication: site and official posts reference the same contract.
  • Rule clarity: program terms published before execution.
  • Phased rollout: features shipped with checkpoints and reporting.

6. Use cases

  • Token-gated access and eligibility-based participation.
  • On-chain incentives with auditable distribution mechanics.
  • Potential payments for drops, tickets, or digital goods (where supported).

7. Security & transparency

  • Verify the contract address and avoid unofficial links.
  • Audits (if any) should be published; absence of audits implies risk.
  • Claims such as “liquidity locked” should include public evidence.

8. Roadmap

  • Phase 1: deployment, onboarding, documentation.
  • Phase 2: community programs and partner activations.
  • Phase 3: token-gated experiences and scaling.

9. Governance

Governance may be introduced with documented proposal rules, voting thresholds, and execution controls (e.g., multisig).
